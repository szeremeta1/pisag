# Legal / Regulatory Notice

**Important:** PISAG is for educational use. Radio transmission is regulated. You are solely responsible for lawful operation.

## Licensing Requirements
- **United States (FCC)**: License required (Amateur, Part 90, experimental). Unauthorized operation can lead to fines and equipment seizure.
- **United Kingdom (Ofcom)**: License required for transmission; amateur or business radio licenses apply.
- **European Union**: Country-specific licensing; consult your national authority.
- **Other Regions**: Check local telecom regulators.

## Frequency Allocation
Paging often uses VHF 138–174 MHz or UHF 400–512 MHz; allocations differ by country. Operate only on licensed/authorized frequencies.

## Power Limits
HackRF outputs low power (~10–15 dBm), but licensing still applies. Comply with your license power limits.

## Interference Risks
Unauthorized transmissions can interfere with emergency, aviation, or commercial services—potentially severe offenses.

## Safe/Educational Use
- Prefer shielded test setups, dummy loads/attenuators.
- Use licensed amateur bands where permitted.
- Maintain logs if required by your license.

## Compliance Checklist
- Obtain the appropriate radio license.
- Verify frequency is permitted for your license and location.
- Set transmit power within licensed limits.
- Use suitable antenna and RF safety practices.
- Keep transmission records as regulations require.
